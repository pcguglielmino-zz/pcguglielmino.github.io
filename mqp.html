Developing Robotic Pollination Drones

A Major Qualifying Project

Why robotic pollination drones

Honey bees are a critical component in modern agriculture, by pollinating flowers they are directly responsible for the produce we eat. Unfortunately, the honey bee population has seen a rapid decline in the past decades. This is due to a variety of reasons such as loss of habitat and pesticides. There is on average a 40% loss in managed bee populations a year, which is quickly becoming unsustainable for farmers. Because of this, my team decided that we want to address this problem with a robotic solution.

What does the project do?

Our project is focused on developing an autonomous drone capable of navigating a given area, locating flowers, and transferring pollen between them using an end effector. This will be accomplished by designing our drone with a variety of sensors and actuators to be able to identify flowers and collect pollen. These sensors will need to be powerful enough to help the drone identify flowers, while also balancing the weight and power requirements of wanting our drone to have a small footprint.

This project is currently in progress, but we are already solving important problems such as designing a robust computer vision system to help our drone identify and target flowers. Our project goals will also include developing a compliant end effector to allow for out drone to collect and deposit pollen from flower to flower. Also, a controller will be developed to coordinate the motion of the drone and end effector with the motion of the flower in order to maximize the amount of pollen collected. Ideally, we will also be able to expand our project towards looking at implementations of swarm robotics with our drone platform.
