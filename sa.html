Robotics Department Student Assistant

Working to give back to the community that has given me so much

Starting my sophomore year, I was chosen to be a Student Assistant for the sophomore level robotics classes. This means that I am responsible for helping sophomore students learn and understand the material in their classes and labs. As a student assistant I have led review sessions in order to assist students in understanding the topics that will be covered before their quizzes and exams. I have also worked with professors and other student assistants to create resources to assist in teaching this material to new student. As a result of the effort that I put into being a student assistant, I consistently get ranked highly on my end of semester surveys that are provided for each SA.

How I try to excel

At the end of last year, I was chosen to be the Head SA for the robotics engineering department at WPI. This means that I would be in charge of making sure that all the SA’s are acting appropriately and handle any concerns from students, professors, or SA’s that need to have a student vouch for them. Unfortunately, due to budget cuts caused by the coronavirus pandemic I was unable to take on the new position and am remaining a student assistant for the sophomore level classes. However, this has not stopped my drive to help the department. As the Rho Beta Epsilon president I also act as a student representative for the department and am using that role to help to produce a survey that can be used to evaluate the entire student body and provide recommendations to the department to help them make decisions going forward.
